# BAVANS Feature Onboarding and Track Separation

## Purpose
Provide a deterministic onboarding capability for new product features while preserving strict education/fiction boundaries.

## Activation Triggers
- New feature, format, or channel is proposed.
- Existing feature expands to another content track.
- Governance flags potential domain crossover risk.

## Inputs
- Company non-negotiables and mission alignment data.
- Product track metadata and current focus directives.
- Feature brief with intended audience, format, and channel plan.

## Operating Workflow
1. Classify feature into educational, fiction, or shared infrastructure scope.
2. Validate boundary rules and prohibited crossover patterns.
3. Define mandatory human checkpoints for irreversible actions.
4. Attach required skills, KPIs, protocols, and workflow dependencies.
5. Produce onboarding decision packet with risks and trade-offs.

## Output Contract
- Feature onboarding decision: approved, deferred, or rejected.
- Required protocol stack and KPI set for safe rollout.
- Explicit separation contract and exception rationale.

## Quality Gates
- No feature passes without explicit track classification.
- No production-impacting activation without human approval.
- Risk trade-offs are mandatory in the final decision packet.
